How to use this app
Upon entering the page, you'll encounter a filled out GeoDash board! You have the option to add or remove from the board, or entirely clear the premade map.

If you wish to customize the GeoDash board, utilize the five buttons beneath the puzzle labeled: Draw, Erase, add spikes, and navigate left or right on the 3 available pages (note, if you want to have more pages. Feel free to pull the GitHub code, and modify the board's width). These buttons allow you to place tiles strategically to make the algorithm have a harder or easier puzzle to solve.

Keep in mind that the starting point is fixed, and will always be the 0 0 (top left corner) on page one. Once satisfied with your GeoDash board, use the 'Start' button to start the AI! When you are finished, use the 'Clear' button to wipe the slate clean and remove all tiles from the map!

What is a Genetic Algorithm?
Genetic Algorithms (GAs) are powerful optimization techniques inspired by the process of natural selection. Similar to how organisms evolve and adapt to their environment, GAs iteratively refine a population of potential solutions to find the most optimal outcome. Each solution, undergoes evaluation based on a fitness function to be selected to produce offspring through crossover and mutation.

This process drives gradually improving solutions over successive generations. Unlike brute-force methods, GAs explore complex solution spaces and handle non-linear, multi-modal objective functions.

GAs are widely applicable across various domains, including engineering, finance, and biology, where traditional optimization methods may struggle. By mimicking the principles of natural selection, GAs offer a versatile and effective approach to solving challenging optimization problems, ensuring adaptability and scalability in tackling real-world scenarios.
